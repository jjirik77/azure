Part 1: Understanding the Ethical Challenges of AI

1. Algorithmic Bias and Fairness

AI can amplify societal biases found in data, leading to unfair outcomes in areas like hiring and justice.

2. Privacy and Surveillance

AI's need for vast data raises concerns about how personal information is collected, used, and protected from misuse.

3. Accountability and the "Black Box" Problem

The complexity of some AI models makes their decisions hard to explain, creating challenges for accountability when errors occur.

4. Job Displacement and Economic Impact

AI automation raises concerns about job loss and economic inequality, requiring strategies for a fair societal transition.

5. Autonomous Systems and Decision-Making

AI in systems like self-driving cars forces us to confront difficult dilemmas, such as how they should act in no-win scenarios.

6. Intellectual Property and Creativity

Generative AI challenges our ideas of authorship and copyright, creating new legal and ethical questions about originality.
